About Janet

Welcome to a world where passion meets resilience. Janet Sherfey, our troupe instructor and creative force, invites you to explore the art of belly dance through her unique lens. With a rich background that spans from accounting to tattoo artistry, and a life story marked by incredible recovery and transformation, Janet embodies the spirit of eclectic adventure. Her book, It's All About Your Belly, is a testament to the healing power of dance and the vibrant journey of life itself.

A Life of Many Passions

With a master’s degree in accounting, Janet’s penchant for organization has led her everywhere from the world’s largest defense contractor to a seafood processing ship on Kodiak Island, Alaska. Owning an ever-present flair for the eclectic, Janet has taken sojourns into many occupations—from driving a team of draft horses to tattoo artistry. Along with achieving fitness certifications in Spinning, Zumba, Hula-Hooping, and Belly Dance (her true love), she is the purveyor of “One of a Kind Costumes,” creating fantasy wear and accessories for events across the country. Accountant, costume designer, goat wrangler, and author, Janet’s life is a roller coaster of passions. Come join the ride!

~ Janet Sherfey, Author of It's All About Your Belly

The Journey of Healing

Janet is a passionate instructor who has dedicated her life to inspiring others. After a life-altering car accident left her wheelchair and walker-bound, belly dancing became her pathway to recovery. The exercise, endurance, and motivation she gained helped her overcome numerous fractures and a heart contusion. This transformative experience inspired her book, and she has since appeared on TV shows across the U.S. to share the healing power of dance.

Leadership at Leonard's Ladies

Ultimately, Janet saved the day many years ago when she stepped in as our current instructor to bring our class back together after a hiatus. She honors the foundation laid by our mentor while bringing her own innovative spirit to the group. She debuted her first original choreography with "Santa Baby" (introducing the boa as a prop) and led us through the memorable Thrill the World choreography. Janet continuously pushes our boundaries by introducing new movement forms including Hula-Hooping, SharQui, and the Essence of Bellydance method.

Teaching Philosophy & Style

Janet's classes cover a wide range of styles, blending traditional Greek, Middle Eastern and Egyptian roots with fusion forms like Country, Hip-Hop, Burlesque, Hula, Tahitian, and Salsa. She integrates yoga and stretching into her classes to enhance flexibility and prevent injury. Her classes often feature traditional accessories such as Isis wings, veils, fan veils, zills, and Tahitian I's. The music is as diverse as her dance style, ranging from traditional belly dance to pop music, and beyond.
